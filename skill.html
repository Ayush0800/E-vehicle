What is an Electric Vehicle (EV)?


An electric vehicle (EV) is a type of vehicle that is powered by one or more electric motors, using electricity stored in rechargeable batteries or another energy storage device. Unlike traditional vehicles that rely on internal combustion engines fueled by gasoline or diesel, electric vehicles operate entirely or partially on electric power. They offer an environmentally friendly alternative to conventional vehicles, producing zero tailpipe emissions during operation, which contributes to reducing air pollution and mitigating climate change. Electric vehicles come in various forms, including battery electric vehicles (BEVs), plug-in hybrid electric vehicles (PHEVs), and hybrid electric vehicles (HEVs), each utilizing different combinations of electric and conventional power sources.

Market on the Move

The Indian EV market is projected to experience explosive growth, with a Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR) ranging from 23.47% to 66.52% depending on the source. This translates to a market value of a staggering $23.51 billion by 2031, according to Straits Research and Fortune Business Insights.

Leading the Charge: Two-Wheelers

Two-wheelers, particularly electric scooters, are currently dominating the EV market in India. Their affordability and growing urban demand make them a popular choice for consumers.

Government Push for EVs

The Indian government is actively promoting electric vehicles. Initiatives like the FAME (Faster Adoption and Manufacturing of Electric Vehicles) schemes offer subsidies and infrastructure development support, paving the way for a smoother transition to electric mobility.
